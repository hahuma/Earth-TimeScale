Paleozoic Era

15 Interestings facts

1_ Life in the sea continued to grow, and tons of fossils for hard-shell animals have been found.
2_ By the end of Ordovician period, plant life moved on land for the first time! This period ended in a mass extinction, the second worst in Earth's history.
3_ Plants and animals no longer needed water to reproduce, allowing them to move and expand on land.
4_ In this era happened the Permian Extinction, 90-95% of all form of life were killed
5_ Many insects and spiders evolved in the Era.
6_ First cockroaches evidences appears during the Carboniferous Period
7_ The Paleozoic Era occurred from about 541 million years ago to about 252 million years ago.
8_ It was a period of time in Earth’s history in which drastic climatic and geological change occurred.
9_ During this period, life began to rebound after the Ordovician–Silurian Extinction Event and Earth began to go from a cold, icy planet to a warmer one.
10_ Sea waters also began to rise after Snowball Earth began thawing.
11_ During this time, all of the continents had formed the supercontinent Pangaea.
12_ One that is called by paleontologists “The Age of the Reptiles” and by paleobotanists “The Age of the Conifer.”
13_ This era is broken down into six distinct periods: the Cambrian, the Ordovician, the Silurian, the Devonian, the Carboniferous and the Permian.
14_ During the Cambrian period the history saw a dramatic increase in the amount of life.
15_ The Ordovician Period is a time in which many different kinds of organisms began forming the familiar biological classes that we are all used to today.
