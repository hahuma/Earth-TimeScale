Archean Eon

15 Interestings facts

1_ The Archean Eon began about 4 billion years ago with the formation of Earth’s crust and extended to the start of the Proterozoic Eon 2.5 billion years ago
2_ Records of Earth’s primitive atmosphere and oceans emerge in the earliest Archean (Eoarchean Era)
3_ Fossil evidence of the earliest primitive life-forms—prokaryotic microbes from the domain called Archaea and bacteria—appears in rocks about 3.5–3.7 billion years old
4_ The start of the Archean Eon is only defined by the isotopic age of the earliest rocks.
5_ The earliest terrestrial materials are not rocks but minerals.
6_ It is thought that the oxygen content in today’s atmosphere must have slowly accumulated through time starting with an atmosphere that was anoxic during Archean times.
7_ Although oxygen did not accumulate in any appreciable amount in the atmosphere until early Proterozoic time
8_ the intense volcanism during the Archean Eon caused CO2 to be highly concentrated in the atmosphere.
9_ Throughout the Archean, oceanic and island arc crust was produced semi-continuously for 1.5 billion years; thus, most Archean rocks are igneous.
10_ Eventually, the ozone layer forms.
11_ As oxygen filled the oceans, it mixed with iron. Iron rusts when it reacts with oxygen.
12_ One type of bacteria present then were the cyanobacteria or blue-green algaes.
13_ The earliest life forms were anaerobic, forms that did not use oxygen to exist
14_ Three billion years ago, the sun was only about 70 percent as bright as it is today.
15_ By the end of the Archaean c. 2500 Ma (million years ago), plate tectonic activity may have been similar to that of the modern Earth.
